# Speech 2: Sympathetic Perspectives Speech "Alternating" Outline

#### Charles Moye

#### Topic: Mass Surveillance

## **Introduction**

1. Introduce your controversy:
    - Divisiveness of the balance between national security and personal privacy in the US
    - Governmental mass surveillance programs arise post-9/11
    - USA PATRIOT Act, Uniting and Strengthening America by Providing Appropriate Tools Required to Intercept and Obstruct Terrorism
        - "Expanded surveillance abilities of law enforcement, including by tapping domestic and international phones, and allowing the search of property and records without owner consent or knowledge." (justice.gov)
    - Faced much criticism in public and in courts, yet got extensions

2. Perspectives and Inherent Issues
    - Perspective
        1. Mass surveillance benefits us
        2. Mass surveillance harms us
    - Inherent Issues
        1. Privacy vs. Security
        2. Government overreach and abuse
        3. Not only governments

3. Map
    - Roots of mass surveillance
    - Trajectory of mass surveillance
    - Reasons its a debate
    - What can be done

4. Transition
    - Americans continue to debate whether or not they agree with the practice of mass surveillance

## **Body**

1. The First Inherent Issue: Privacy vs. Security
    - Perspective 1
        - Forfeitting privacy for collective security is necessary
        - Also acceptable if you have nothing to hide
    - Perspective 2
        - Individual privacy is a fundamental right
        - Standing by as entities harvest data may prove detrimental to future freedom
    - Transition
        - Government use of data

2. The Second Inherent Issue: Government Overreach and Abuse
    - Perspective 1
        - Oversight and legal constraints prevent abuse
        - Made to target threats, not typical citizens
    - Perspective 2
        - History of government misuse of surveillance data
            - Warrantless wiretapping
            - Warrantless data collection
        - Unchecked authority
    - Transition
        - Beyond government surveillance programs, other entities also engage in mass surveillance

3. The Third Inherent Issue: Not Only Governments Engage in Mass Surveillance
    - Perspective 1
        - Private corporations use surveillance for security and business optimization
        - They use surveillance for customer safety and security, i.e. fraud prevention
    - Perspective 2
        - Corporations collecting and monetizing personal data
            - Loss of privacy
        - Corporations lack accountability mechanisms found in government
    - Transition
        - Despite the divide, there must be a balance of safety, individual freedoms, and peace of mind

## **Conclusion**

1. Summarize main points
    - 25 years ago -> today, the bigger picture
    - End goal
    - Finding the balance

2. Identify the stasis
    - Determine how much surveillance is necessary and justifiable for our security while maintaining fundamental privacy rights

3. Closing Technique
    - Where do you stand on the scale?
    - As an individual you can find your own balance
        - It will require sacrifice
    - Is it even worth it?
